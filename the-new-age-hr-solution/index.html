2020 - UX and UI Design

End-to-end designs for a New-Age HR Solution

The Project and My Role

360 feedback solutions that exist today tend to do more for management and less for the employee using it. We wanted to shift this paradigm since the most value is found when the employee can understand how to implement the given feedback and improve. This was the gap we wanted to fill with our product. The product would help employees understand their strengths and weaknesses, and then guide them on overcoming their weaknesses.

There were four major sections to the product,

  • Assessment - For the user to identify strengths and weaknesses
  • Education - For the user to consume curated content on their own.
  • Exposure - For the user to connect with others and get mentored
  • Experience - Allowing the user to implement what they have learned in real-world scenarios.

I was part of a 5 member team, insisting of 2 co-founders with HR domain knowledge, and 2 developers. I was the sole designer on the project.

The Team

I worked on this project from May 2020 to July 2020.

My role included,

  • Requirement collection and design project planning
  • Leading the project kick-off and conducting structured ideation sessions with the co-founders
  • Creating User Flows
  • Wireframing and Interface Design
  • Co-ordinating with Developers to share assets and testing the final product for design suggestions

One key philosophy we followed throughout the design process was that we didn't want this to be dull like other HR apps in the market. We wanted users to be excited in using this product. We applied Yu-kai Chou's Gamification & Behavioral Design Framework to come up with ideas to improve engagement on the app.

Constraints

Since this was a self-funded venture by the co-founders, timelines were strict and we needed to be as frugal as possible. Due to this, minimal user research was conducted from my end and I relied on insights from the market research that the founders that previously conducted.

Also, I borrowed references from other products were necessary, but did not conduct a thorough Competitor Analysis on this project.

The product was to be tailored as a desktop-only app to begin with given the demographic and use case.

Challenges

Since this product had the complexity of 4 different apps, I wanted to design a framework in which all these functions could sit well.

I had to create a design system since this was a new product.

Since the assessments (Questionnaires) in the product were lengthy and complex, the instructions for the assessment needed to be clearly explained to the user, and the result of the assessment had to be presented in a layman manner.

Lastly had to keep the founders mindful of the timelines we were working under, and keep every brainstorming and ideation session structured and meaningful.

The User

The user was a 20-45 year old MNC employee. Their aspirations were to do their job better, get promoted, and be admired by their managers and peers.

To achieve these aspirations, they needed to understand how to improve, be guided, and to be educated in soft-skills like negotiation, and conflict resolution.

The user was motivated to do this by their aspirations, peer pressure, and support from their manager.

Wireframing

I took over three weeks in this stage. We wanted to be extremely thorough in this stage to make sure that we were building consistent and scalable systems.

We constantly reiterated through the wireframes and ensured that any new design element was thoroughly vented and reasoned for.

We started by creating a broad framework for the home page that adapted as the user went through the various stages of the program.

Each section had it propose that was clearly defined so that the section was not misused in the future and the user knew what to expect always.

Simplifying Assessments

One critical piece that would determine the success of the product would be the assessments phase.

During this phase, the user would fill a bunch of forms in order to understand themselves better. This data would determine the educational content that would be shown to the user, as well as match them with the relevant users in the organisation to improve more.

The design challenge was to make these forms more interesting so that there would be good adoption of the product.

Since this was the first phase in their journey we wanted to make a good impact and provide hand holding through this journey.

There were 6 different assessments that the user had to take. We wanted to create a framework that worked across these so we could have consistency and save on dev time.

Visual Design

Since this was a new product, there hadn’t been any branding exercise done before. To tackle this problem, I created a moodboard and had the founders pick the directions that most appealed to them and their close group of collegues (who would be the early users of this product).

Moodboard

Design System

I wanted to make sure right from the start that there was maximum reusability of components and consistency in the UI wherever possible.

I made sure that minimal number of new components we added on as we tackled each usecase.

I made full use of Figma’s Component building capabilities to ensure that any designer could build on this system in the future and use existing components with ease.

Final Designs

The final step was creating all the pages and assets for the development team. I worked closely with the development team to make sure that there was clarity in the designs that were handed over. Also, we were able to make do with minimal screen designs since it was modular and we had to work with states of each modular component. We also made Figma the single source of truth for the developers.

Impact

The product was designed in 2 months from concept to developer ready assets.

The process that we followed allowed us to unblock developers very soon in the journey so was to minimum are to market speed.

The product is currently in Beta. The founders are onboarding their first set of 200 employees on the platform.
