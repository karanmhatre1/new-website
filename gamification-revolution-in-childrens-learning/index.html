Gamification Revolution in Children's Learning

The Project and My Role

WhiteHatJR is a platform where children learn how to code. We wanted the children to have fun while learning.

The objective was to improve class completion rates, renewal rates, and project completion rates using gamification.

There were three aspects to this,

  • Points - Students gained points for each activity they performed on the platform.
  • Badges - For special activities or activity streaks, students earned badges.
  • Leaderboard - Students competed against each other on a weekly leaderboard.

I was part of a 3 member team, insisting of two product managers, one UX writer, and me as the designer.

The Team

I worked on this project for 3 months from July 2020 to October 2021.

My role included,

  • Ideation with the Product team
  • User Interviews
  • Wireframing and Interface Design
  • Illustration
  • User Testing
  • Co-ordinating with Developers to share assets and testing the final product for design suggestions

Challenges

We started only with a broad idea on what we wanted to implement. We had to work closely with the product manager to while creating the requirements.

Since this is a children's product, we needed to design without our own biases and constantly test it with children to make sure that the visual and language are appealing to them.

Points

Like any game, we first needed a basic currency unit. For us, it was points. Students get points when they do activities on the platform (such as attend a class, or complete an assignment). We wanted to find a good represention for points, as well as show how many points a student will get for each acitivity and use that as an incentive to get students to complete certain activities.

Badges

Badges helped us promote certain activities. The design challenge with badges was to first understand what type of badges appealed most to children between 6-14 years of age. We created a mood board and tested different themes and illustration styles.

There were three foundational designs to be created,

  • Illustrating the badges
  • Showcasing badges
  • Promote badges on various activity pages
  • Celebrate a student winning a badge

Leaderboard

We wanted to launch a leaderboard that gave the student a sense of progression and kept them wanting more even after winning.

We wanted to create a leveling system, where each week that the student finished in the top 10 of the leaderboard, then the student was moved to a higher league.

We made some quick mockups and reached out to students. Student interviews revealed two major problems,

  • Students failed to understand that they needed to maintain their position in the top 10 till the end of the week. The assumption was that as soon as they entered the top 10 they would be moved to the next level.
  • New Students did not understand how to earn points.

We iterated over the mockups and content a few more times and tested with students until we were satisfied with the layout and direction. Learnings and decisions based on the reasearch,

  • Introduce onboarding slides to build the narrative for the student
  • Finalising on the theme of “Mountains”, since students found it interesting and clearly understood progression across the levels.
  • Show tasks that the student can complete in order to earn points.

Impact and Future Scope

At first we decided to measure the impact of gamification based on renewal rates and we saw that students that participated regularly in the leaderboard saw a higher renewal rate. But, this could be attributed to causation rather than correlation.

We interviewed students a week after the feature was released and it revealed that students understood and enjoyed collecting points and badges.

Some students were very competitive on the leaderboard and we received suggestions from parents to reveal the breakdown of how other students were earning points so there would be more transparency.

We saw an uptick in Projects being completed on time, as well as Quiz completions.

Students were completing the leaderboard leagues faster than we enticipated, and we soon had to introduce addtional leagues.

We later added a Points MarketPlace which helped boost the value of the points and improved student’s engagement with the platform even further.
