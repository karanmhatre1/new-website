Through my career I have worn many hats - a front-end developer, a product manager, and a now a product designer.

My versatility in skill-sets gives me a great perspective when it comes to product thinking and problem solving.

I have worked across small and large teams to deliver impact fuel products in both the B2B, and B2C industries. I am passionate about UX Research, and UX Design, and have experience working in the Healthcare and Education space.

I am a quick learner and a generalist. I'm interested in the topics of Education, Art, and Creative Coding.

One thing that has always remained at the heart of this journey has been creating engaging and delightful digital experiences for users.

Currently, I work with Byju’s (India’s Leading EduTech Startup) as a Lead Product Designer. At Byju’s I design products for 6 to 14 years ago students to attend live classes. This includes the video classroom experience, and the student dashboard experience.

I believe that great products are built on the foundation of structured data analysis and user research.

I did my undergrad in Computer Engineering.

Through college I freelanced and built websites for friends and family. This was when I discovered my love for creating digital products. I really enjoyed designing and coding these websites.

Me along with a team of 2 created a Timetable generator using Genetic Algorithms as part of our final year project. We were able to implement it in our college for the teacher as well.

After Engineering, I freelanced as a creative developer for two years (2012-2014).

During this time I worked with clients like Haiku Travels, Gibson India, Tata LitLive Festival, NH7 (Now PayTM Insider), AFK Gaming, and Phantom Films, to create engaging web experiences for them.

The scope of work for these projects was pretty much everything. From scoping the product, to design, to building database architecture, to development of the complete portal, to deployment and early maintenance.

In 2014, I joined a small 3-member digital agency named Genii in Mumbai as a front-end developer.

At Genii I worked as a fullstack developer (Front end development and Ruby on Rails) where I slowly transitioned into a UX Designer. I helped them hire a team of five UX Designers and set up UX processes.

This was very early in my career. Here I got guidance from the founders on how to improve my skills not only as a UX Designer, but also as a Manager. I quickly got involved in sales, managing clients, and leading projects end-to-end.

In 2017, the agency was acqui-hired by Practo Technologies (at the time India’s leading Healthcare Startup).

We also improved of search ranking by generating content related to common treatments and surgeries.
  • Later, the teams were restructured and I was made the Product Manager on the “Practo Tab” product - an android tablet-based clinic management (SAAS) product that eased the work of a receptionist at a doctor’s clinic. During this time I launched a Consent Form feature that let doctors collect and store consent signatures from patients.

In May 2017, I joined GOQii Technologies as a Senior Product Manager.

GOQii connects, tracks and takes care of your entire family’s health through a smart preventive eco-system and GOQii Care team.

In 2015, GOQii had a 20-member product team with 4 Product Managers. It had been a 5 year old startup that had raised Series B funding.

The product was a range of Fitness Bands and a mobile app that let the user get access to Fitness Coaching.

I worked directly with the CEO. It was a fast paced enviornment with quick product releases.

I was tasked with handling the experience for the Fitness Coaches as well as the coach interaction on the mobile app. We responsibilities included,

  • Understanding the requirements from the coaching team and delivering the right features.
  • Understanding the customer's pain points and using the coaching platform to solve for those.
  • Understanding the business goals and implementing unique incentive structures for the coaches to help us achieve these goals.

We managed to improve coach efficiency - Coaching in 2015 were managing 100-120 users each. We were able to move that metric to 250 by 2017. This was done by using a hybrid chat model, where nudges and reminders were automatically sent my the system instead of the coach. We also added better user data views on the coaching dashboard that helped the coach right out to the rright users without wasting too much time deep diving through data.

The second project I was part of at GOQii was the India Health Quiz. We had partnered with Indian Celebrity Akshay Kumar to launch a ATL media campaign for GOQii. The center piece of the campaign was Busting Health Related Myths with the help of the India Health Quiz on the GOQii app.

After 5 years of Product Management, I decided I wanted to pursue UX Design again.

There were a few reasons for this change,

  • I wanted to be close to the Product and the Users again. As you scale higher in the Product Manager Career Map, the focus moves towards business and financial goals. While I understand why this is important for any company, I believe my passion and expertise lie in Product Thinking.
  • In my PRDs I noticed that there was an overemphasis on UX Design. I loved to create wireframes and prototypes for my products. It helped me think more clearly. In my free time, I would read UX related articles, and rarely found interest in business articles. I didn’t see myself becoming the best Product Manager in the world, but I did see myself becoming the best UX Designer.
  • It was the right time in my career to manage this shift. I believed that my Product Management experience would make me a sharper UX Designer. It was time.
